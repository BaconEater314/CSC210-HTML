Status Effects

Scarlet Rot

Scarlet Rot is a dangerous affliction that whittles away the afflictedâ€™s health. Scarlet Rot deals exclusively Scarlet Rot damage. A creature can withstand a number of Scarlet Rot buildup equal to its Constitution score + its level. When that number is reached or exceeded, it loses all Scarlet Rot buildup and is Poisoned. While poisoned this way, a creature takes 2d10 scarlet rot damage at the start of each of its turns for the next minute. Spells that remove the Poisoned condition do not remove Scarlet Rot unless they specify that they do so.

Blood Loss

Blood Loss is an affliction that builds up over time. A creature can withstand a number of Blood Loss buildup equal to its Constitution score + its level. When that number is reached or exceeded, it loses all Blood Loss buildup and loses 10% of its maximum hit points. A creature loses Blood Loss buildup upon receiving healing.

Madness

Madness is an extremely dangerous affliction that causes characters to go mad. A creature can withstand a number of Madness buildup equal to its Intelligence score + its level. When that number is reached or exceeded, it loses all Madness buildup and gains a form of short-term madness (pg. 259 of the DMG). A creature that has madness can make an Intelligence Saving throw against the DC of the effect that caused the madness at the end of each of its turns, ending the madness on a success.

Damage Types

Black Fire Damage

Black Fire damage is a subtype of fire damage. Creatures immune to fire damage take only 25% of Black Fire damage, and creatures with fire resistance take 75% of Black Fire damage.

Scarlet Rot Damage

Scarlet Rot damage is a subtype of poison damage. Creatures immune to poison damage (excluding constructs) take only 25% of Scarlet Rot damage, and creatures with poison resistance take 75% of Scarlet Rot damage.

Ancient Dragon Lightning Damage (AD Lightning)

AD Lightning damage is a subtype of lightning damage. Creatures immune to lightning damage take only 25% of AD Lightning damage, and creatures with lightning resistance take 75% of AD Lightning damage.

Schools of Magic

Sorceries

Sorceries are a unique type of school of magic for the Elden Spells. All sorceries are on the spell lists for the Sorcerer, Warlock, and Wizard, with a few on the Artificer, Bard, Cleric, and Druid lists.

Sorceries come in several different varieties:

  • Carian
  • Cold
  • Crystalian
  • Dragon Cult
  • Glintstone
  • Gravity
  • Magma
  • Moon
  • Sellian

Incantations

Incantations are a unique type of school of magic for the Elden Spells. All incantations are on the Cleric and Druid spell lists, with a few on the Artificer, Bard, Paladin, Ranger, Sorcerer, Warlock, and Wizard.

Incantations come in several different varieties:

  • Beastial
  • Black Flame
  • Blood Oath
  • Dragon Cult
  • Erdtree
  • Frenzied Flame
  • Giant's Flame
  • Golden Order
  • Servants of Rot
  • Two Fingers
