Properties

Properties for all the weapons used.

  • Ammunition: You can use a weapon that has the Ammunition property to make a ranged attack only if you have ammunition to fire from it. The type of ammunition required is specified with the weapon's range. Drawing the ammunition is part of the attack (you need a free hand to load a one-handed weapon).
    • Tracking Ammunition: Each attack expends one piece of ammunition. After a fight, you can spend 1 minute to recover half the ammunition (round down) you used in the fight; the rest is lost.
  • Autofire: As an action, spray bullets in a 10-foot-cube area within normal range. Each creature in the area must succeed on a Dexterity saving throw or take the weapon's normal damage.
    • The DC is 8 + Proficiency Bonus + Dex mod.
      • Creatures not proficient with firearms do not add PB to the DC.
    • Autofire costs 10 bullets unless specified
  • Burst Fire: As an action, you may fire a number of shots equal to your proficiency bonus in rapid succession, with disadvantage on every attack beyond your normal amount. You may also dive up to 10 feet to the side without provoking opportunity attacks, landing prone. You cannot fire more shots than what are loaded.
  • Concealable: Weapons with the concealable property can be easily concealed from visual searching. Physical, in-depth searching? Not much will get past that anyway.
  • Finesse: When making an attack with a Finesse weapon, use your choice of your Strength or Dexterity modifier for the attack and damage rolls. You must use the same modifier for both rolls.
  • Heavy: You have Disadvantage on attack rolls with a Heavy weapon if it's a Melee weapon and your Strength score isn't at least 13 or if it's a Ranged weapon and your Dexterity score isn't at least 13 unless it is mounted on a surface. A small-sized creature's strength must be 16 or greater to wield these weapons effectively.
    • Extremely Heavy: Weapons with this property require a Strength of 15 to wield properly unless it is mounted on a surface. Creatures that do not meet the required Strength cannot wield this weapon while it is not mounted on a surface. A small-sized creature's strength must be 20 or greater to wield these weapons effectively.
  • Mountable: Weapons with this property come with a bipod that allows them to be mounted on surfaces as a bonus action. While mounted, creatures ignore the strength requirement to use them, and they cannot move more than 5 feet away from the surface their weapon is mounted on until they use a bonus action to unmount or leave it behind.
    • A creature can drop prone and mount a weapon on a flat portion of a floor at the same level as them.
  • Loading: You can fire only one piece of ammunition from a Loading weapon when you use an action, a Bonus Action, or a Reaction to fire it, regardless of the number of attacks you can normally make.
  • Lunge: Once per turn, when making an attack, you may lunge forward with this weapon, increasing your reach by 5 feet for this attack. On a hit, deal 1d6 + your ability modifier Piercing damage on a hit.
  • Range: A Range weapon has a range in parentheses after the Ammunition or Thrown property. The range lists two numbers. The first is the weapon’s normal range in feet, and the second is the weapon’s long range. When attacking a target beyond normal range, you have Disadvantage on the attack roll. You can't attack a target beyond the long range.
  • Reach: A Reach weapon adds 5 feet to your reach when you attack with it, as well as when determining your reach for Opportunity Attacks with it.
  • Static Pump: As a bonus action, you may pump the Lever Action Rifle an additional time, generating extra static electricity. Multiply the damage by 1.3 (rounding up) if you hit. Static electricity dissipates over time, meaning you can save this pump for your next turn, but no longer. Extra pumps while this is in effect add no extra benefits.
  • Thrown: If a weapon has the Thrown property, you can throw the weapon to make a ranged attack, and you can draw that weapon as part of the attack. If the weapon is a Melee weapon, use the same ability modifier for the attack and damage rolls that you use for a melee attack with that weapon.
  • Two-HandedA Two-Handed weapon requires two hands when you attack with it.
  • UndisarmableThis weapon cannot be disarmed while you are wielding it.
  • VersatileA Versatile weapon can be used with one or two hands. A damage value in parentheses appears with the property. The weapon deals that damage when used with two hands to make a melee attack.
  • Precision Weapon: Precision weapons have disadvantage on attack rolls against creatures within 30 feet.
  • Special:
    • Arc Thrower: Can instead attack all creatures in a 15ft cone originating from the weapon. Doesn’t add Dex modifier to damage. All creatures in the cone must make a Dexterity saving throw, taking half damage on a success.
      • The DC is 8 + Proficiency Bonus + Dex mod.
        • Creatures not proficient with firearms do not add PB to the DC.
    • Chakram: Ranged attacks in melee range do not have disadvantage. When making ranged attacks against creatures within 5 feet, the weapon can be caught after the attack.
    • Double-Bladed Scimitar: After making the attack action, you can immediately use your bonus action to make a melee attack dealing 1d4 + your ability modifier Slashing damage. Weapon Mastery (Nick) makes this attack free.
    • Katana: The Katana uses Strength when wielded with 2 hands
    • LMG: Creatures that fail the saving throw against the LMG’s autofire take half damage.
    • Net: A Large or smaller creature hit by a net is restrained until it is freed. A net has no effect on creatures that are formless, or creatures that are Huge or larger. A creature can use its action to make a DC 10 Strength check, freeing itself or another creature within its reach on a success. Dealing 5 slashing damage to the net (AC 10) also frees the creature without harming it, ending the effect and destroying the net.
    • Nunchucks: Once per turn, after making a melee attack with this weapon, you may make an additional melee attack. It deals 1d6 Bludgeoning damage.
    • Shuriken: After making the attack action, you can immediately use your bonus action to make a ranged attack dealing 1d4 + your ability modifier Slashing damage.
    • Shocker Sidearm: The Shocker Sidearm cannot be detected at all when concealed.
    • War Staff: After making the attack action, you can immediately use your bonus action to make a melee attack dealing 1d6 + your ability modifier Slashing damage. Weapon Mastery (Nick) makes this attack free.

Weapon Masteries

Weapon Mastery is a feature exclusively for martial classes. Starting at level one, Barbarians, Blood Hunters, Monks, Paladins, Rangers, and Rogues get 2 weapon masteries. Fighters get 3 at level 1, 4 at level 4, 5 at level 10, and 6 at level 16. These do not stack through Multiclassing.

During a long rest, you can swap out 1 Weapon Mastery.

Weapons have 2 Master Properties listed; choose one of the two options you have Mastery in. You cannot have both at the same time.

Properites Description
Cleave If you hit a creature with an attack roll using this weapon, you can make an attack roll with the weapon against a second creature within 5 feet of the first that is also within your reach/range. On a hit, the second creature takes the weapon’s damage, but you don't add your ability modifier to that damage unless that modifier is negative. You can make this extra attack only once per turn.
Disarm Once per turn, when you hit a creature with this weapon, you can force the creature to make a Strength saving throw (DC 8 plus the ability modifier used to make the attack roll + Proficiency Bonus). On a failed save, the creature drops one object of your choice that it is holding, with the object landing in its space.
Graze If your attack roll with this weapon misses a creature, you can deal damage to that creature equal to the ability modifier you used to make the attack roll. This damage is the same type dealt by the weapon, and the damage can be increased only by increasing the ability modifier or magic weapon modifiers.
Nick When you make the extra attack of the Light property, you can make it as part of the Attack action instead of as a Bonus Action. You can make this extra attack only once per turn.
Poison Once per turn, when you hit a creature with this weapon, you can force the creature to make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 plus the ability modifier used to make the attack roll + Proficiency Bonus). On a failed save, the creature is poisoned until the end of its next turn.
Push If you hit a creature with this weapon, you can push the creature up to 10 feet straight away from yourself if it is Large or smaller.
Sap If you hit a creature with this weapon, that creature has Disadvantage on its next attack roll before the start of your next turn.
Slow If you hit a creature with this weapon and deal damage to it, you can reduce its Speed by 10 feet until the start of your next turn. If the creature is hit more than once by weapons that have this property, the Speed reduction doesn’t exceed 10 feet.
Topple If you hit a creature with this weapon, you can force the creature to make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 plus the ability modifier used to make the attack roll and your Proficiency Bonus). On a failed save, the creature has the Prone condition.
Vex If you hit a creature with this weapon and deal damage to the creature, you have Advantage on your next attack roll against that creature before the end of your next turn.
Zap Once per turn, when you hit a creature with this weapon, you can force the creature to make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 plus the ability modifier used to make the attack roll + Proficiency Bonus). On a failed save, loses one reaction.
